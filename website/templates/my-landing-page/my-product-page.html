About

Nutritonal labels haven't changed for 30 years, but nutritional science has changed a lot. Scientists have discovered over 80 essential nutrients that people should be getting every day. How many people even know that? Many food and drinks aren't even required to have a nutritional label, or have information that is so lacking in detail other than 'calories'. Most of the fruit and fresh produce won't have any labels. The same goes for the alcohol. If people saw how much sugar was in alcohol, people would probably think again about drinking it! But even the people who know are concerned about their diet, it can be so overwhelming to push that desire into action. The mammoth task of researching all of the micronutrients, figuring how much you need based on your age, sex and personal medical issues and then finding the foods that fit in a meal plan that are tasty but also not too expensive, and not too time-consuming to cook... Oh gosh, I'm getting stressed just thinking about it! Well, like many, I have been there, but I want to change that, and I want to make it as easy as possible for anyone to eat healthy! I believe empowering users with data and knowledge will encourage healthy eating and make it accessible to everyone.

Our Story

My name is David, I am a student who loves solving problems and helping people change their lives. I am also a software developer. When I started university, the responsibility of cooking and preparing food for myself was thrown into my hands. I loved cooking and trying out fun recipes that my family wouldn't usually cook. However, as I researched more into food and diet, I realised that I should probably be more thoughtful about what I eat. I would spend hours skimming through Healthline articles, making tables of nutritional information, to try and figure out a meal plan that works for me and meets all my nutritional needs. I had a friend who also share this interested, to an even greater extent than me, who started reading scientific papers on nutrition and different nutrients.
